Predicting NHL Goal Scorers: A Rare Event Classification Approach

By Mohammad G. Nasiri | Work in Progress

1. Problem Definition

The Tim Hortons NHL Hockey Challenge requires selecting three players who will score a goal in upcoming games. From a data science perspective, this is an imbalanced binary classification problem, as the probability of a specific player scoring in a specific game is typically low (< 15%).

2. Methodology

I utilized the NHL Public API to gather historical data. The modeling strategy involves:

  • Feature Engineering: Calculating rolling averages of goals, shots on goal, and time on ice over the last 5 games.
  • Model Selection: Using XGBoost due to its efficiency with tabular data and ability to handle missing values.
  • Handling Imbalance: adjusting scale_pos_weight to penalize false negatives more heavily.

3. Next Steps

The current model achieves an AUC of 0.72. Future work includes integrating player fatigue metrics (back-to-back games) and goalkeeper statistics.
